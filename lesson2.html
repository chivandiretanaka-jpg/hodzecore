Lesson 2 — Variables

In this lesson you will learn what a variable is and how to use one in your program. This is one of the most important ideas in all of coding.

What is a variable?

A variable is a box that stores information. You give the box a name, and you put something inside it. Your program can then use that information whenever it needs it.


Example: Imagine a box called name. You put the word Tanaka inside it. Now whenever your program needs your name, it just opens that box.

Your challenge

Use Blockly to create a variable, store your name in it, and print it to the screen.

  • Click Variables on the left panel
  • Click Create variable and name it: myName
  • Drag the set myName block into the workspace
  • Connect a Text block and type your name inside it
  • Add a print block and connect the myName variable to it
  • Click Run — your name should appear in the output
